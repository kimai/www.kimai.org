What recognition includes

Earned standing, not a signup perk

Every element below follows from being recognised — it reflects demonstrated competence and continued good standing, and is withdrawn if that standing lapses.

Directory listing

A profile in the public partner directory, surfaced to organisations actively looking for trusted help with Kimai.

Early roadmap access

Briefings on what's coming next, so recognised partners can plan, prepare and advise their clients ahead of release.

Official badge & logo usage

The right to display the Official Kimai Partner mark on your site and proposals — a clear, verifiable signal of your standing.

Qualified lead referrals

Introductions to organisations whose needs match your track, region and expertise. Referrals reflect fit, not favours.

The relationship, in plain terms

What recognition is — and isn't

{% capture text %} Recognition as an Official Kimai Partner is **non-exclusive**. Kimai may recognise any number of partners in the same region or specialism, and partners remain free to work with other software and vendors. The designation is **reviewed periodically**. It reflects current standing rather than a permanent title, and is **revocable** — for misrepresentation of the relationship, a sustained drop in quality, or conduct that breaches the program's expectations. Recognition does **not** create an agency, reseller, franchise or employment relationship. Partners act independently, contract directly with their own clients, and speak for themselves rather than for Kimai. There is **no commission, revenue share or referral fee** in either direction. Partners keep what they earn from their own work; Kimai takes nothing from it. The program recognises competence — it is not a financial arrangement. {% endcapture %} {{ text|markdownify }}
